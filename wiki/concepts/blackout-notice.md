# Blackout Notice

A required participant communication created by the [[com]] that informs plan participants of the period during which they cannot make changes to their accounts while assets are being transferred.

## Definition

During a plan conversion, there is a window — the "blackout period" — when participant investment elections, loans, withdrawals, and other account changes are locked while assets move from the prior record keeper to Transamerica. Federal regulations require that participants receive advance notice of this blackout period.

The COM creates the blackout notice, routes it to the plan sponsor for review and approval, and then sends it through [[ellen-miller]] for mailing to participants.

## Why It Matters

Blackout notices are legally required and time-sensitive. Participants must receive them far enough in advance of the blackout start date. COM must coordinate blackout dates with the prior record keeper and ensure the notice is approved and mailed with adequate lead time.

The COM main checklist tracks blackout dates as a field within the Prior Recordkeeper Info section — confirming that blackout timing is part of the prior RK coordination workflow, not just the participant communications workflow.

## Workflow

1. COM coordinates blackout dates with prior record keeper
2. COM creates blackout notice
3. Sent to plan sponsor for review and approval
4. Plan sponsor approves
5. Notice mailing file created
6. Notice request routed to [[ellen-miller]] for mailing
