# PRD — Plan Review Document

A detailed document comparing the prior provider's plan design to the TransAmerica plan design. Reviewed internally first, then with the client, then signed.

## Overview

The PRD is one of two main plan specification documents used during onboarding (the other being the [[onboarding-package]]). It details plan provisions — eligibility rules, vesting schedules, contribution sources, and anything special about how the plan is structured.

## DC-Relevant Sections

The PRD covers a lot of territory. Most sections are for other departments. DC focuses on:

- **Eligibility** — service requirements, hours thresholds, entry dates
- **Vesting** — vesting schedules by source
- **Sources** — which sources exist, any frozen sources, any new sources being added
- **Special strategies** — unusual conversion approaches, frozen fund handling

Other sections (distributions, deferral elections, etc.) belong to other departments and are generally not the DC's concern.

## Authorship

The **COM creates the PRD** using a standard TA template. It is not provided by the prior record keeper.

## Process

1. COM prepares the PRD from the standard TA template
2. **Internal review:** DC and relevant teams go over it in an internal meeting
3. **External review:** COM presents to client in an external meeting
4. **Client feedback:** Any corrections or requested changes are made
5. **Client signs** the PRD — this is one of the first things done when the plan begins

> The PRD is signed early — well in advance of liquidation. Do not expect to be waiting on it near conversion time.

## If PRD and P3 Setup Are Inconsistent

If the DC notices a discrepancy between what the PRD says and what's set up in P3:
- Alert the **COM** and **Doc Ops**
- They coordinate the fix
- DC does not resolve PRD/P3 conflicts unilaterally

## Amendments

The PRD can be amended after signing, but only in **very special circumstances**. Not a standard occurrence.

## Relationship to Onboarding Package

Some plans use a [[onboarding-package]] instead of a PRD. PEP plans (Pooled Employer Plans) always use onboarding packages. All other plan types use the PRD. The two are mutually exclusive — a plan uses one or the other, not both.

## What Happens After Signing

Once signed, the PRD serves as the authoritative reference for plan provisions during the entire conversion. It informs:
- [[base-file]] field selection
- [[p3]] setup verification (classes, eligibility rules, sources)
- [[source-mapping]] (which sources to map)
- [[fund-mapping]] (which funds are active vs. frozen)
