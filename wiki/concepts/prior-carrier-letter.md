# Prior Carrier Letter

A formal letter authored by the [[com]] notifying the prior record keeper that their plan is transitioning to Transamerica. Sent to the plan sponsor and used to notify the prior carrier directly.

## Definition

Once the conversion is underway, the COM creates a letter addressed to the prior record keeper formally announcing the plan transition. The letter is first sent to the plan sponsor (client) and the prior carrier is notified as part of this process.

A **Successor Trustee Letter** may also be created if specifically requested — this is a separate, related document covering trustee transition.

## Why It Matters

Formal notification to the prior carrier is a required step in the transition. Without it, the prior carrier may not initiate document transfers, blackout setup, or liquidation coordination. This letter is one of the earliest touchpoints with the prior RK.

## Workflow

1. COM creates the prior carrier letter
2. COM sends to plan sponsor (client) for awareness
3. Prior carrier is notified (directly)
4. Successor Trustee Letter created separately if requested (via letter request to Plan Services cc)
