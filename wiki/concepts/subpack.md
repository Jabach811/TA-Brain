# Subpack — Submission Package

TransAmerica's internal document specifying exactly what will be done during the conversion — the "how we're doing it" complement to the PRD's "what the plan looks like."

## Overview

While the [[prd]] and [[onboarding-package]] describe the plan's design from the client's perspective, the subpack describes TransAmerica's execution plan: which mapping strategy, what auto-enrollment rate, how funds will be handled, etc. It is an internal TA document, not client-facing.

## Contents (Examples)

- Deferral election mapping strategy ("we will map elections 1:1")
- Auto-enrollment percentage (e.g., "auto enroll at 1%")
- Fund transfer strategy ("transfer in kind: 3 funds; cash convert: remaining")
- Any special processing instructions

## Ownership

**COM creates the subpack. TC takes over after reviewing it during the internal call.**

The COM checklist explicitly includes:
1. Submission Package Created (COM)
2. Distribute to entire internal project team (COM)
3. Subpack review — TC reviews during the internal call
4. Subpack hand-off — TC maintains the subpack after review

In practice, completion has been inconsistent from the DC's perspective.

> "Sub-packs have been kind of touch and go, hit or miss." — DC Brain Dump

When the subpack is missing or incomplete, the most likely explanation is that COM created it but the distribution or review step broke down — not necessarily that it was never made. DCs should ask COM directly.

## Importance

The subpack is valuable because it gives the DC a single document stating definitively what will happen. Without it, the DC must synthesize conversion strategy from multiple sources, increasing the risk of misunderstanding.

## When the Subpack Is Missing

Not a blocker. The information exists somewhere — in the [[prd]], [[onboarding-package]], from the COM verbally, or from the client. Ask the COM for the specific details you need.

> "Get the info from other places. Not a big deal. It's available somewhere or from someone."

## Additional Clarifications

- **Standard template:** Yes, exists.
- **Ideal timing:** Should be complete at the very beginning of the conversion — before substantive DC work begins.
- **Client visibility:** The client knows about it, but it is primarily an internal TA document.
