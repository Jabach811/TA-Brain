# Matt O'Connell

Leads the team responsible for setting up Fidelity pooled accounts for Transfer In Kind (re-registration) conversions.

## Overview

When a plan conversion includes funds being transferred in kind (shares moved directly rather than liquidated), Matt O'Connell's team sets up the receiving accounts at Fidelity. These accounts are pooled (not per-participant) and are identified by expected share amounts rather than account numbers.

## Key Facts

- Team setup time: approximately **10 business days**
- Accounts are set up at **Fidelity** as a shared pool
- Identification method: expected share amounts per fund (not account numbers)
- Team begins monitoring and reporting **immediately** upon setup — do not start too early
- Receives the **re-registration form** filled out by the DC
- Receives updated share amount estimates from the DC (from TOA initially, then test files, then final files)

## Timing Rules

> Do NOT engage Matt's team too early. Once set up, they immediately begin reporting on expected vs. received shares. Starting a month early creates weeks of "nothing here" noise.

Recommended timing:
- Take the **liquidation date**
- Subtract **10 business days** (Matt's team setup time)
- Subtract **1–2 more days** as buffer
- That is the target date to send the re-registration form

Goal: accounts should be complete approximately **1–1.5 weeks before liquidation**.

## Workflow with DC

1. DC fills out the re-registration form (triggered by TOA column T = "Y")
2. DC sends form to Matt O'Connell's team
3. DC provides estimated share totals (from TOA current values — may be old)
4. DC updates totals after test files arrive
5. DC updates totals immediately when final files arrive — **time-sensitive**
6. Matt's team identifies incoming shares by matching expected amounts
7. Matt's team pulls shares into plan accounts once identified

## Communication Method

Matt's team communicates via a **shared spreadsheet**. DC fills in expected share amounts when final files arrive; Matt's team updates it when shares are received at Fidelity. DC monitors the spreadsheet to know when all shares have arrived.

## Share Amount Mismatch

If shares don't match expected amounts: DC investigates and contacts the prior vendor directly.

## TIK Tracker Spreadsheet

Yes — there is a separate **TIK tracker spreadsheet** from the re-registration form. DC fills in expected shares per fund when final files arrive. The re-reg form is the setup document; the tracker is the monitoring tool during the TIK transfer period.
