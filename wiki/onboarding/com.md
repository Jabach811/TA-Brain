# Onboarding Guide: Communications Coordinator (COM)

## Who This Is For

A new Communications Coordinator joining the Large Market team at Transamerica. This guide assumes no prior knowledge of TA systems or processes and is oriented around what you actually need to do in your first weeks on the job.

## Reading Order

### Foundation — What You're Walking Into

1. **[[overview]]** — What TA's conversion business is, at the highest level. Read this first to understand the domain before any detail.
2. **[[plan-conversion-handoffs]]** — The master map of how work moves between all teams. This is your operating context: everything you do lives inside this flow.
3. **[[internal-teams]]** — Who all the internal players are. COM touches every team; know them all by name before your first kickoff.
4. **[[conversion-types]]** — Cash vs. mapping vs. transfer in kind. Every conversion is one of these; know the difference early.

### Your Role

5. **[[com]]** — Your own role page. Read the full 18-phase workflow. This is the checklist translated into reference prose — understand every phase before you manage one.
6. **[[nsa]]** — The trigger that starts everything for you. Know what it is and what you do the moment you receive it.
7. **[[project-plan-com]]** — Your first deliverable after NSA receipt. Two formats: PEP vs. non-PEP.
8. **[[nbi]]** — You upload the project plan here and complete the audit checklist here at go-live. Understand how NBI is used across all teams, not just by you.

### Documents You Own or Route

9. **[[prd]]** — COM authors the PRD. This is the plan design document that the DC, TC, and QA all read. You need to know what's in it and who needs it.
10. **[[subpack]]** — COM creates the subpack for non-PEP plans; TC takes it over after review. Know what it contains and why it matters to the DC.
11. **[[onboarding-package]]** — PEP-plan equivalent of the PRD. COM manages the tabs, PEA, and Info Sharing Form.
12. **[[toa]]** — Transfer of Assets. Client-signed fund map. Relevant to your OBP workflow (PEP) and the DC's fund mapping workflow.
13. **[[prior-carrier-letter]]** — You create this; know what triggers it and where it goes.
14. **[[blackout-notice]]** — Legally required participant notice. Time-sensitive. Know the routing chain.
15. **[[vef]]** — Vendor Expense Form. If applicable; know the 6-step routing chain through advisor → client → L&C.

### Payroll Coordination

16. **[[contribution-monitoring-form]]** — TFS plan payroll setup form. Involves a TC AWD flip that you must trigger.
17. **[[rdd-form]]** — TC-originated, client-completed, COM-routed. Simple loop but easy to let slip.
18. **[[ftp-connection]]** — The payroll vendor's file upload channel. Set up by FTP Team; you need to know this is a separate dependency from payroll test files.

### Go-Live

19. **[[early-access]]** — Pre-go-live participant window. COM owns the coordination; know when to request it, who activates it, and how tight the timing is.
20. **[[csr]]** — Conversion Summary Report from the Quality Consultant. You receive it and send it to the client as part of go-live close-out.
21. **[[audit-pack]]** — DC-built post-conversion summary. Complements (or precedes) the CSR. QA reviews it. Know it exists so you can reference it if clients ask for post-conversion documentation.

### Key People to Know Immediately

22. **[[ellen-miller]]** — You will route prior documents through her, send notice mailing requests through her, check SF plan setup with her, and notify her when the plan is live. Central relationship.
23. **[[jen-curtin]]** — You notify her for 360° payroll plans. Understand her role as head of the FTP team.
24. **[[lm-dc]]** — The Data Consultant is your closest internal partner. They confirm asset receipt, provide early access templates, and receive payroll questionnaires from you. Read the DC role page to understand what they need and when.
25. **[[tc]]** — Transition Consultant. They review your subpack, provide your QDIA and fee disclosures, flip AWD for contribution monitoring, activate early access, and typically set up the go-live review. Deeply intertwined with your workflow.

## Key People to Meet

- Your assigned DC for each plan — relationship is plan-by-plan
- TC on your team — the go-live review depends on them setting it up
- Ellen Miller — central routing contact; introduce yourself early
- Jen Curtin — relevant as soon as you have a 360° payroll plan
- The Quality Consultant on your team — they produce the CSR you'll deliver to clients

## First Week Priorities

1. Get access to NBI and locate the project plan upload workflow
2. Understand the PEP vs. non-PEP split — almost every document decision flows from plan type
3. Read an existing project plan for a plan already in progress to understand the format
4. Shadow a kickoff call (internal and external) before running one yourself
5. Identify your TC and DC counterparts for any plans being assigned to you
