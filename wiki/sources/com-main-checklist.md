# Source: COM Main Checklist

A structured web-based checklist application used by Communications Coordinators (COMs) to track every task across a plan conversion lifecycle — from NSA receipt through go-live.

## Summary

This is the authoritative task list that defines the COM role in operational terms. It is organized into 18 phases covering kickoff, prior recordkeeper coordination, letters and notices, payroll setup, document execution, early access, and go-live. Two plan-type branches exist throughout: PEP (Pooled Employee Plan) and Non-PEP, with certain sections exclusive to each.

The checklist reveals that the COM role is far more than a meeting scheduler — COMs are the document factory, the routing hub, and the go-live coordinator for every plan conversion.

## Key Claims

1. **NSA (New Sale Announcement) is the trigger** — the COM workflow begins when the NSA is received.
2. **COM creates and owns the project plan** — uploaded to NBI and sent to the conversion team.
3. **COM is the letters-and-notices factory** — responsible for cover letters, blackout notices, mapping notices, QDIA initial notices (sourced from TC), fee disclosures, privacy notices, and conversion newsletters.
4. **COM manages the prior RK document chain** — requests prior plan docs, routes them through Ellen Miller to the docs team, and coordinates prior carrier letters and successor trustee letters.
5. **COM co-owns payroll coordination** — sends specs, runs payroll calls, manages questionnaires with clients and DCs, handles Contribution Monitoring Forms (which require TC to flip an AWD setting), and manages the RDD Form loop with TC.
6. **Subpack ownership is COM → TC** — COM creates the subpack; TC reviews it during the internal call and takes over maintenance after that.
7. **Early access is COM-coordinated** — COM requests the template from DC, routes it to the client, submits back to DC, and notifies TC to activate it.
8. **Go-live is COM-orchestrated** — COM sends the go-live email, completes the NBI audit checklist, completes the Auto Delivery Form (non-PEP), adds the plan to the Survey Spreadsheet, and notifies Ellen Miller.
9. **CSR (Conversion Summary Report) comes from the Quality Consultant** — COM receives it and forwards to the client.
10. **PEP plans have additional document requirements** — OBP, PEA, Info Sharing Form (via DocuSign), plus AAA, Secure 2.0, and SPD from the docs team.
